About Me

Hi, I'm Nereo — half German, half Mexican, based in Frankfurt am Main, husband, dad, and someone who somehow found himself diving headfirst into the Matrix of web development and AI (yes, I know… "Nereo" and "Neo" — destiny had jokes).

I didn't always walk the straight path. I used to drift, hang out, and waste too many nights chasing nothing. Life wasn't easy, and I didn't always make the best choices. But somewhere along the way, I decided to step out of that loop — to become someone my family could rely on, and someone I could respect in the mirror. I learned that even if you start off in the wrong direction, you can still rewrite your story and evolve.

Curiosity is my code

By day, I work full-time in freight forwarding at Frankfurt Airport, where logistics, precision, and timing rule the game. By night (or whenever life allows), I explore the worlds of web design, coding, and digital creativity — fueled by equal parts curiosity and good background music.

I'm currently studying Marketing & Digital Media, and what fascinates me most is how technology, design, and storytelling connect people. The more I learn about HTML, JavaScript, Python, and AI, the more I realize it's not just about writing code — it's about understanding how ideas come to life in a digital space.

Why this website exists

This site isn't a business — for now. It's a sandbox for learning, testing, and documenting progress. I want to be honest about where this might lead: once I've built real skills and experience, I'd like to use them to eventually step outside the 9-to-5 "Matrix." Until then, this space remains a personal, non-commercial project focused on curiosity and practice. I have to be careful what I write here. The tax office in Germany is like Agent Smith...

What I'm hoping for

This is primarily a space for me — a quiet place to learn at my own pace, keep notes, and track experiments. If you happen to stumble in and find something useful or interesting, that's great, but I'm not trying to turn this into a community hub or conversation pit.

And if some of the code looks like it was written under the influence of "vibe-coding" after a long day of work, studies, and family life — you're absolutely right.

In short

I'm someone who loves technology, storytelling, and music. I believe in evolving and improving, no matter how old I am — progress over perfection, always.

So, welcome to my little corner of the web. If you've read this far, chances are we already have something in common.
